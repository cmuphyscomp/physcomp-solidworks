The gear profiles in this folder were generated using the free gear profile generator tool at http://hessmer.org/gears/InvoluteSpurGearBuilder.html.

The parameters were chosen to correspond to common "24 pitch" gears with 14.5 degree pressure angle, although the actual DXFs use metric units. These gears have has 24 teeth per 1 inch of pitch circle diameter.

The pitch circle circumference is the pitch diameter multiplied pi = 1.0 * pi = pi.

The circular pitch p is the distance between teeth along the pitch circle:

      p  = (1.0 * pi) / 24
      = 0.1308996938995747 inches
      = 3.3248522250491974 mm

The gear profiles were generated using the following parameters:

      3.32485 mm	circular pitch
      14.5 degree	pressure angle
      0.1 mm		clearance
      0.1 mm		backlash
      0.0 mm		profile shift
      6.35 mm		center hole (1/4 inch)

DXF profiles were generated as follows:

      teeth	pitch diameter
      84	3.50 inch      
      24	1.00 inch
      18	0.75 inch
      12	0.50 inch

Note: the files were generated in millimeters but describe a standard inch gear size. Such is life in the USA: the rest of the world just uses millimeters now, but inch-sized parts are easier and cheaper to buy here.
